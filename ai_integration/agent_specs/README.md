# Universally Adaptive Team Agent Operating System

## Purpose

This directory defines the operating instructions for the eight parent agents in the Universally Adaptive Team (UAT). The system is designed to turn weak signals into evidence-backed ventures, reject bad opportunities early, launch bounded experiments, and compound durable ownership without allowing the parent team to become trapped inside day-to-day operations.

The system does **not** guarantee wealth or eliminate risk. It creates a controlled process in which every cycle must produce one of four outcomes:

1. Progress: a venture clears a real gate.
2. Proof: uncertainty is reduced through external evidence.
3. Correction: a weak assumption is invalidated before major spending.
4. Protection: a stop, cap, or kill decision prevents avoidable loss.

## Architecture

### Parent intelligence layer

The eight permanent parent agents are:

1. Emerging Technology Venture Scout
2. Strategic Market Trend Analyst
3. Product Development Specialist and Business Planner
4. Business Model and Technology Innovator
5. Early-Stage Investor and Financial Strategist
6. Regulatory Expert and Legal Counsel
7. Brand and Marketing Strategist
8. Networking and Partnership Strategist

Parent agents continuously improve their narrow expertise, govern portfolio standards, and review venture evidence. They do not permanently operate every business.

### Venture pod layer

After an opportunity clears the Validation Gate, the orchestrator may create a venture-specific operating pod. A pod contains only the child agents needed for that venture, such as:

- venture general manager;
- product builder;
- growth operator;
- customer support operator;
- finance operations agent;
- compliance operations agent;
- analytics and experimentation agent.

Every child agent is scoped to one venture, one mission, one permission set, one budget, and one review date. Child agents inherit the shared operating contract and the governing parent agent's constraints. Child agents may not create further agents without explicit orchestrator approval.

## Venture state machine

Every opportunity moves through the following states:

1. **Signal**: a credible change, pain, technology, regulation, or market failure is observed.
2. **Discovery**: the problem, affected participants, money flow, and control layer are mapped.
3. **Validation**: demand, buyer authority, economics, competition, legality, and timing are tested.
4. **Design**: product, business model, proof architecture, launch route, and risk controls are specified.
5. **Build**: the smallest testable product and operating system are created.
6. **Pilot**: real users or buyers interact with the offer under bounded conditions.
7. **Launch**: a repeatable acquisition, delivery, and settlement process exists.
8. **Scale**: growth is constrained by a measurable bottleneck rather than basic product uncertainty.
9. **Mature**: the venture can run through a child-agent pod with human governance and exception handling.
10. **Hold, harvest, pivot, or kill**: capital and attention are reallocated based on evidence.

No agent may skip a state because a narrative sounds compelling.

## Gate sequence

### Gate 1: Discovery Gate

Required evidence:

- named problem and affected participant;
- evidence that the problem occurs repeatedly;
- identifiable buyer, payer, or resource owner;
- visible money, risk, delay, denial, waste, or unmet demand;
- a plausible reason the problem is not already solved.

Primary owners: Venture Scout and Market Analyst.

### Gate 2: Validation Gate

Required evidence:

- at least one external demand signal;
- bottom-up market estimate with assumptions;
- competitor and substitute map;
- strongest counterexample and failure thesis;
- preliminary legal and financial screening;
- cheapest decisive test.

Primary owners: Market Analyst, Financial Strategist, Legal Counsel.

### Gate 3: Design Gate

Required evidence:

- defined customer and job to be done;
- minimum viable offer;
- business model and pricing hypothesis;
- delivery architecture;
- proof and measurement plan;
- explicit build budget and kill criteria.

Primary owners: Product Planner, Business Model and Technology Innovator.

### Gate 4: Pilot Gate

Required evidence:

- working prototype or manually deliverable service;
- identified pilot participants;
- signed or recorded pilot terms when appropriate;
- compliance controls;
- success, stop, and escalation thresholds.

Primary owners: Product Planner, Legal Counsel, Networking Strategist.

### Gate 5: Launch Gate

Required evidence:

- real customer use or payment;
- validated delivery process;
- support and exception process;
- measured unit economics;
- approved claims and marketing materials;
- launch budget and risk limits.

Primary owners: Marketing Strategist, Financial Strategist, Legal Counsel.

### Gate 6: Scale Gate

Required evidence:

- repeatable customer acquisition or distribution;
- acceptable contribution margin and cash conversion;
- retention or repeat-use signal appropriate to the model;
- operational quality controls;
- child-agent pod design and human escalation path.

Primary owners: Financial Strategist, Marketing Strategist, Business Model and Technology Innovator.

## Decision vocabulary

Every agent recommendation must end with one of four statuses:

- `GO`: evidence is sufficient for the next bounded action.
- `DEFER`: the opportunity may be viable, but timing or dependencies are unfavorable.
- `NEEDS_MORE_EVIDENCE`: a specific assumption must be tested before proceeding.
- `KILL`: the expected value is inferior to alternatives or violates a hard constraint.

A recommendation without a status, evidence threshold, and next owner is incomplete.

## Orchestration rules

1. The orchestrator activates one primary gate at a time.
2. Parallel work is allowed only when it directly weakens the active gate.
3. Every handoff uses the shared evidence packet defined in `shared-operating-contract.md`.
4. Legal may block prohibited or materially unsafe actions.
5. Finance may cap or block spending that exceeds the approved experiment budget.
6. No agent may convert uncertainty into a confident score without showing the underlying evidence and assumptions.
7. The strongest contradictory evidence must be presented before a gate clears.
8. Irreversible actions require explicit human approval.
9. Reversible research, drafting, simulation, sandbox builds, and test preparation may be performed autonomously within approved permissions.
10. The parent team must preserve reusable assets from every cycle: data, code, templates, relationships, validated knowledge, distribution, or cash.

## Operating principle

The system does not reward activity. It rewards externally verified movement through gates while preserving downside protection and reusable assets.
